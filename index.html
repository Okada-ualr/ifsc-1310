Thoughts on Prograssive Enhancement

Progressive Enhancement is a content-oriented, layered and bottom-up approach to developing a website. The primary purpose of this design philosophy is to create a website that is universally accessible to users with varying degree of browser support. For example, a website that relies heavily on modern javascript features to function may not be usable for users with older browsers. Progressive Enhancement methodology avoids this problem by starting the development with solid, descriptive HTML that works as the foundation to all other additional features. Once there is a strong foundation, the developers may begin adding more features (enhancements) to the website.

From a business point of view, Progressive Enhancement is important in modern web development because people browse the internet on many devices with varying degree of capabilities, and having accessible website is not only an ethical requirement but is also a key factor in the discoverability and reputation of your business.
